📣 What to say at the door ▾

Open: “Hi, I’m [name], a neighbor here in the Town of Middleton. I’m not selling anything — just gathering signatures on [the issue]. Got 30 seconds?”

The point: “Basically [what it does, one sentence]. We need signatures from Town residents to put candidates on the ballot for the open MCPASD school board seats.”

The ask: “Are you a registered voter here in the Town? Great — would you add your signature?” (Hand them the clipboard, pen ready.)

Close: “Thank you! Know any neighbors who’d care about this?”

More info: “Want the full details? Everything’s at openseatsmcpasd.org — there’s an FAQ, who’s running, and how to get involved.”

If they hesitate:
• “Busy” → “Just your name & address, 15 seconds.”
• “Does it obligate me?” → “No — it just asks that this be considered.”
• “I don’t know enough” → “Signing means residents get a say, not that you’ve decided.”
• Disagree? → “Thanks for hearing me out!” Move on.

⚠ Make signatures count: signer must be a registered voter in the Town of Middleton and should sign in front of you. Get a legible name, full address, and date. No home? Leave a door hanger and tap “Not home.”
